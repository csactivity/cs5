" Carry On. "

When you look back, you will see and realize why things had to be like that, to be in that shape and why they had to happen the way they did. There are just some things and people who are only meant to be part in that particular page and will no longer be seen and written in the succeeding pages, but they always leave something in you.

No matter how much you try to relive and linger, you need to accept and let go—the world will not stop on moving forward even if you do. If you feel stuck at the moment, know that there is a way out, so keep on walking regardless of how uncertain you are of the path. It's okay to look back and get a glimpse of what you have lost along the way, but always see and look forward to what will be waiting for you ahead. Thus, you must not also forget to keep the journey at your own pace.

by Jaeka (18) (18)

"The Story of the King Who Owned Wild Dogs "

Tap here to see the story!

The late king of a certain community had ten wild dogs. He used them to torture and eat any of his servants who made a mistake. One of the servants gave a wrong opinion, and the king didn’t like it at all. So he ordered that the servant be thrown to the dogs.
The servant said, “I served you for ten years, and you do this to me? Please give me ten days before throwing me to those dogs!” The king agreed.
In those ten days, the servant went to the guard who looks after the dogs and told him he would like to serve the dogs for the next ten days. The guard was baffled but agreed, and the servant started feeding the dogs, cleaning them, bathing them, and providing all sorts of comfort for them.
When the ten days were over, the king ordered that the servant be thrown to the dogs for his punishment. When he was thrown in, we were all amazed to see the ravenous dogs only licking the feet of the servant! The king, baffled at what he was seeing, said, ”What has happened to my dogs?”
The servant replied, "I served the dogs for only ten days, and they didn’t forget my service. Yet I served you for a whole ten years and you forgot all, at my first mistake!" The king realized his mistake and ordered the servant to be set free.

Uchechukwu Ajuzieogu (25) (25)
